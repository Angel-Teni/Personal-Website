About Me

I'm a creative technologist who believes the best solutions emerge at the intersection of imagination and technical precision. As a Computer Science student at UT Austin with a passion for both full-stack development and cybersecurity, I approach every challenge as an opportunity to craft something meaningful, secure, and user-centered.

My journey into tech has been shaped by a unique blend of interests. With a minor in Statistics and Data Science and a certificate in Digital Arts and Media, I don't just write code—I design experiences. Whether I'm building a web application to help foster youth find resources or developing secure operating system components, I bring a designer's eye for detail and a developer's commitment to functionality.

In the realm of full-stack development, I thrive on transforming ideas into tangible, interactive applications. From crafting intuitive front-end interfaces to architecting robust back-end systems, I love the entire journey of bringing a product to life. But what truly drives me is ensuring that these systems are not just functional, but fundamentally secure. Cybersecurity isn't an afterthought in my work—it's woven into every line of code, every architectural decision, every user interaction.

As someone who mentors 100+ students in Java programming and serves as Public Relations Officer for the Association of Black Computer Scientists, I'm committed to fostering inclusive tech communities where creativity and security-minded thinking go hand in hand. I believe the future of technology belongs to those who can think both creatively and critically about the systems we build.
